PAGE 1 – THE WALK-UP

Wide establishing shot. The family approaches a three-story Victorian house sagging under decades of neglect. Paint peels in long curls, windows glow faint orange, jack-o-lanterns line the cracked stone path with carved faces that seem to watch. You wear a torn gray hoodie, green zombie makeup dripping at the edges, phone flashlight cutting a white cone ahead. Mom adjusts a tall black witch hat over curled hair, green face paint, oversized purse slung cross-body. Natalie twirls in a sleek black catsuit, velvet cat ears, long tail swaying, phone in selfie position. Trevor trails behind in a shiny red vampire cape two sizes too big, plastic fangs already slipping, collar popped high.
Halloween night, 11:47 PM – one minute to curfew.
Medium shot, front porch. The front door hangs open six inches, warm light spilling onto warped floorboards. Fake blood drips from the brass knob in glossy streaks. A hand-painted sign reads “ENTER IF YOU DARE” in letters that look freshly wet.
Interior foyer, wide angle. They step inside. Dust floats in shafts of orange light from a chandelier draped in gauze webs. Plastic skeletons dangle from the ceiling on nearly invisible fishing line. A grandfather clock ticks too loud. Wallpaper peels in floral strips revealing horsehair plaster.
Tight shot on the door behind them. The heavy oak door swings shut on its own. The latch clicks with finality. All four heads turn at once.

PAGE 2 – LOCKED IN

Medium shot. You rattle the doorknob—solid, no give. Behind you, tall windows slam downward in perfect sync. Wooden boards slide across them from the outside and nail themselves in place with metallic hammering.
Close-up on Mom’s purse. Mom digs inside, pulling out pepper spray, car keys, a crumpled IHOP coupon. The coupon suddenly ignites in her fingers, curling to ash.
Wide downward angle. Overhead lights dim to blood red. Floorboards glow, forming a perfect pentagram that pulses like a heartbeat. Dust swirls upward in reverse.
Full splash, bottom half. Thick black smoke erupts from the pentagram seams. A seven-foot demon rises—charred skin, curling ram horns, yellow eyes with vertical slits, chains dangling from wrists thick as tree trunks. The air ripples with heat.

PAGE 3 – PANIC AND PLAN

Wide action shot. The demon lunges. You all scatter in four directions. Cape fabric rips as Trevor dives left. Natalie flips over a velvet chaise. Mom swings her purse like a morning star.
Split panel, left. Natalie crouches behind an open coffin lined in purple satin. Her phone screen shows 1,200 live viewers. She mouths silently to the camera, thumbs-up.
“1.2K viewers—drop a like if we die.”
Split panel, right. Mom stands on a Persian rug, purse open. Fun-size candy bars rain out as she swings it at the demon’s knee. The demon swats; a Snickers bar explodes into chocolate dust.
Medium shot on pedestal. You skid to a stop in front of a waist-high stone pedestal. An ancient leather-bound book lies open, pages yellowed, ink glowing faintly.
Close-up on your face. Your eyes widen. You spin toward Trevor, who is cornered against a suit of armor.

PAGE 4 – THE BARGAIN

Wide shot. Trevor fumbles the plastic fangs out of his mouth, holding them up like a peace offering. Drool string connects fang to lip for a second before snapping.
Close-up on demon. The demon leans in, nostrils flaring. Its pupils dilate into cartoon hearts. A tiny pink tongue flicks out.
Transformation sequence (three mini-panels stacked). Demon shrinks, horns retract, skin smooths to dull gray, chains evaporate. A three-foot imp now stands, cradling the fangs like treasure. Name tag: “HI, I’M KEVIN.”
Wide shot. Portals iris open in every wall—swirling purple light. The front door unlocks with an audible click. Dust settles.
Freedom smells like burnt toast and IHOP syrup.

PAGE 5 – THE ESCAPE

Exterior night, medium shot. The family bursts out the front door in a tangle of limbs. Trevor’s cape is inside-out. Natalie still live-streams, running backward. Mom clutches her now-empty purse like a shield.
Wide overhead. Behind them, the entire house folds in on itself like cardboard scenery on a stage. Roof caves, walls accordion, until only a flat painted backdrop remains leaning against nothing.
Close-up on the imp. The imp waves from the lawn, plastic fangs now mounted on a tiny velvet pillow. It blows a kiss.
Final wide shot, dawn creeping. The four of you walk away down the sidewalk. Streetlights flicker off one by one. Your zombie makeup has smeared into tired raccoon eyes. Mom’s witch hat is crooked. Natalie’s tail drags. Trevor’s cape is a sad red puddle around his shoulders.
Happy Halloween—tip your demons.

PAGE 6 – BONUS EPILOGUE

Interior IHOP, 12:03 AM. Fluorescent lights, sticky booths. The family sits around a table piled with pancakes. You pour syrup in the shape of a pentagram by accident. Natalie films a slow-motion syrup pour. Mom guards a fresh stack of fun-size candy like gold. Trevor finally gets his new fangs to stay in—upside down.
The imp’s velvet pillow sits in the corner booth, fangs proudly displayed next to a half-eaten smiley-face pancake.
